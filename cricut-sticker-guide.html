⚠️
Inkjet Printers Only Cricut Printable Sticker Paper must only be used with inkjet printers. A laser printer generates heat that will damage both the sticker paper and your printer.
📐
About Sheet Size Cricut Printable Sticker Paper is 8.5" × 11" (US Letter) or A4. Design Space manages the printable area automatically — if any sticker exceeds the cuttable boundary, a red ⚠ icon appears in the Layers panel and you'll be prompted to resize. No manual calculation needed.
📋
About the Cutting Mat — Important For this project you need a 12" × 12" mat. Here's what to use:

✅ Best: LightGrip mat (blue) — specifically recommended for sticker paper and lightweight materials. Holds the sheet securely during cutting and releases it cleanly without tearing.

⚠️ If you only have a StandardGrip mat (green): It will cut correctly, but the stronger adhesive risks tearing or curling the sticker sheet when you remove it — especially if the mat is new. Two ways to reduce this risk: (1) before loading the sticker sheet, press a clean piece of scrap paper onto the mat and peel it off several times to reduce the tackiness; (2) after cutting, always flip the mat face-down onto a flat surface and peel the mat away from the sheet rather than lifting the sheet off the mat. A well-used green mat that has lost some stickiness is usually fine.

❌ Do not use: StrongGrip (purple) or FabricGrip (pink) mats — both will damage the sticker paper.

Step-by-Step Instructions
Expand each step for full detail
🚨
Do This First — Every Time After a New Machine Setup Without a completed calibration, the Cricut sensor cannot correctly locate registration marks and cuts will be misaligned. Design Space may also block you from proceeding until calibration is done.

Path in Design Space (Desktop):

🗺️
Navigation Menu (☰ top-left) → Settings → Machines → select your Cricut Maker 3 → Machine Calibration → Start → select Print Then Cut
  1. Open Design Space and ensure your Cricut Maker 3 is connected.
  2. Open the ☰ Menu → Settings → Machines → select your machine → Machine Calibration → Start → choose Print Then Cut.
  3. Select your printer from the dropdown and click Print. Design Space will send a calibration sheet to your inkjet printer — print it on plain paper.
  4. Place the printed calibration sheet on a cutting mat (either LightGrip or StandardGrip is fine — this is plain paper), aligned to the top-left corner.
  5. Load the mat and press the flashing Go button. The machine will scan the sheet and make test cuts. Do not unload the mat.
  6. Examine the small square cut in the centre. Design Space asks: "Does the cut touch the printed line all the way around?"
    • Yes → Proceed to fine calibration (the machine will make additional cuts along the top and right edge).
    • No → Design Space runs a basic calibration — tell it which numbered/lettered lines align best, then reprint a fresh sheet and repeat.
  7. For fine calibration: examine the grid of cuts along the top and right side of the page. Tell Design Space which numbered line (top) and lettered line (right side) sits closest to the printed line.
  8. The machine will make a final confirmation cut around the large outer rectangle. If it aligns, confirm Yes. Calibration complete! If not, run through the process once more — a second attempt almost always resolves it.
✅
Success Check The final large rectangle cut should track exactly along the printed black line all the way around. Once confirmed, you won't need to re-calibrate unless cuts start drifting again.

📖 Full official guide: Calibrating your machine for Print Then Cut — Cricut Help Center ↗

  1. In Design Space, click Upload (left toolbar).
  2. Click Upload Image → Browse and select the student's PNG file.
  3. If prompted to select an image type, choose Complex (best for detailed PNG artwork). Note: newer versions of Design Space may skip this screen and proceed directly to the background editing step — if you don't see it, just continue.
  4. On the next screen, check the background:
    • If the background is transparent (checkerboard pattern): proceed directly.
    • If there's an unwanted background colour: use the background removal tools on this screen to clean it up before saving.
  5. Click Save as Print Then Cut Image — this is essential. Saving as a cut-only file would strip all colour.
  6. The image appears in your Uploads library. Click it to select it, then click Add to Canvas (sometimes labelled Insert Images depending on your Design Space version) to place it on the canvas.
  7. Repeat for every student's PNG.
💡
Check the Layers Panel After inserting, each image should appear in the Layers panel with a printer icon (🖨) and show Print Then Cut as the operation. If it shows just a scissors icon, it's a cut-only file — delete it and re-upload, saving as Print Then Cut.
  1. Each uploaded image is automatically placed on the canvas when inserted. Drag it by clicking and dragging directly on the canvas to position it freely. No grid or fixed layout is required.
  2. Resize each sticker individually to suit the design — stickers can all be different sizes. Use the corner handles and keep the lock ratio enabled to avoid distortion.
  3. Ensure there is clear visible spacing between all stickers. They must not overlap or touch, or the cut paths will interfere with each other.
  4. If the red ⚠ icon appears in the Layers panel next to a sticker, the overall layout is too large. Click the icon and choose Auto-Resize, or reduce individual sticker sizes until the warning clears.
📐
Layout Tip Design Space manages the printable boundary automatically. If a red exclamation mark appears beside a layer, it means stickers are exceeding the cut area — simply reduce sizes or remove one sticker until the warning disappears.
ℹ️
What the Offset Does The Offset tool generates a slightly enlarged shape around the sticker image. When the kiss cut follows this offset shape rather than the image edge, the sticker has a visible white border — giving it a polished, "sticker-sheet" look when peeled.
  1. Select the student's PNG on the canvas.
  2. Click Offset in the top Edit toolbar.
  3. Drag the slider or type a value to set border thickness (0.05"–0.1" is typical for a clean look).
  4. Choose rounded corners for a smooth outline. Click Apply.
  5. The offset shape appears as a separate layer in the Layers panel. Set its fill colour to White — this ensures the white border prints visibly on the sticker paper and that the kiss cut follows the offset outline rather than the inner image edge.
  6. Repeat individually for each sticker that needs a border.
⚠️
Don't Offset All Stickers Together Apply the Offset to one sticker at a time. Selecting all stickers before offsetting merges them, making it impossible to move individual stickers around the canvas afterwards.

Rectangle Layer
Outer card boundary — full die-cut through backing
Die Cuts
  1. Click Shapes (left toolbar) → select Rectangle. A rectangle appears on canvas.
  2. Resize and position it to form the outer boundary of the sticker card for one student. The rectangle should surround the sticker image with a comfortable margin.
  3. In the Layers panel, set the rectangle's operation to Basic Cut (not Print Then Cut). Its fill colour doesn't matter — it won't print, only cut.
  4. In the Layers panel, drag the rectangle entry to the bottom of the layer list (below the PNG and offset entries). In Design Space, lower in the list = further back on the canvas. The correct order from top to bottom of the list is: PNG (top) → Offset → Rectangle (bottom).
  5. Repeat: add a fresh rectangle for every student's sticker.
💡
Rectangle size is fully flexible Each student's card can be a different size. The rectangle is the final product boundary — make it as large or small as looks good for that student's design.
🚨
If You Skip This Step — The Whole Sheet Will Fail Without Attach, Design Space repositions each layer independently on the mat. The print and the cut paths will be completely out of alignment. Every sticker group must be Attached before printing.
  1. Click on the PNG image for one student's sticker on the canvas.
  2. Hold Shift and also click the offset shape (if used) and the rectangle for that same sticker.
  3. In the Layers panel on the right, scroll down and click Attach.
  4. The three layers now move and cut as one locked group. In the Layers panel, they'll appear indented under an Attach group header.
  5. Repeat for every student's sticker group on the canvas — one Attach action per student.
📚 Flatten vs. Attach — Know the Difference
ToolWhat it doesUse in this workflow?
Attach Locks the position of multiple layers relative to each other on the mat, while keeping each layer's cut/print operation separate. The PNG still prints; the rectangle still cuts; the offset still kiss-cuts — all precisely aligned. ✔ Yes — use this
Flatten Merges all selected layers into a single printable layer. All distinct cut operations are lost — you end up with one image that prints and cuts around its own outer edge. Useful for simple single-sticker sheets, but wrong here because it removes the separate rectangle die-cut instruction. ✘ Not for this workflow

📖 Official reference: How to Print Then Cut in Design Space — Cricut Help Center ↗

Run through this checklist before clicking Make:

  1. All stickers are positioned within the canvas boundary — no part of any sticker or rectangle extends outside the printable area. No red ⚠ warnings in the Layers panel.
  2. There is adequate spacing between all stickers (and their rectangles). They must not overlap or touch.
  3. In the Layers panel, every sticker group shows as an Attach group containing its PNG, offset (if used), and rectangle.
  4. No stray rectangles, offsets, or shapes are floating alone on the canvas.
  5. Double-check that each PNG layer is labelled as Print Then Cut in the Layers panel (printer icon 🖨).
✅
You're ready when All layers are attached, all stickers are inside the canvas, no warnings appear, and all PNGs show as Print Then Cut. Proceed to Make.
  1. Click Make (top-right of canvas).
  2. On the Prepare screen, verify the mat preview looks correct — all stickers visible, properly spaced.
  3. Click Continue.
  4. On the Make screen, click Send to Printer.
  5. In the Print Setup dialog:
    • Add Bleed: If your sticker has a white offset border, toggle this Off — the border provides the margin and bleed is not needed. If any sticker's design runs right to the cut edge (no offset applied), leave this On to prevent a white sliver appearing at the cut line.
    • Check Use System Dialog — this opens your printer's own settings window.
  6. In the system print dialog, set quality to High / Best and paper type to Matte Photo Paper or closest equivalent. This gives the sharpest, most vibrant print.
  7. Load the Cricut Printable Sticker Paper into the printer — check your printer's manual or settings for which way to orient the printable side, as this varies by printer model and tray. If unsure, print a test page on plain paper first to confirm orientation. Load only one sheet at a time.
  8. Click Print. Do not touch the printed sheet until the ink is fully dry (30–60 seconds).
  9. You will see a thick black registration box printed around the sheet — this is correct and essential. The Cricut uses these marks to locate where to cut.
⚠️
Do Not Touch the Registration Border The black border around the sheet smudges easily when fresh. Handle by the edges only after printing, and allow it to fully dry before placing on the mat.
  1. Place the printed sticker sheet on your cutting mat, aligned precisely to the top-left corner. Smooth it down firmly with your hand or a brayer — no bubbles or lifting edges.
    • LightGrip (blue) mat: Ideal for sticker paper — use as normal.
    • StandardGrip (green) mat: Will work, but if the mat is new or very sticky, first reduce its tack by pressing and peeling a scrap piece of paper on it a few times before loading the sticker sheet.
  2. Back in Design Space, on the Make screen, select the material: click Browse All Materials and search for Printable Sticker Paper, White. Select the correct version:
    • Gray Liner Printing — older Cricut sticker paper (thick, with gray grid on liner)
    • Green Liner Printing — newer Cricut sticker paper (thinner liner, less likely to jam)
  3. Load the mat into the Cricut Maker 3: align it with the left guide rails and press the Load/Unload button (double arrows icon) until the mat is gripped.
  4. Press the flashing Start button. The machine will:
    • First (automatic): Scan the registration marks in the corners of the printed sheet to locate the design precisely — no cutting yet.
    • Cut pass 1: Kiss-cut around each sticker (or offset shape) — cuts through the sticker layer only, leaving the backing intact.
    • Cut pass 2: Die-cut along each rectangle — cuts fully through both sticker paper and backing to create separate cards.
  5. When the machine stops and the Load/Unload button flashes, press it to unload the mat.
  6. Before removing the sheet from the mat, check one sticker: lift a corner to confirm the kiss cut didn't go through the backing. If the rectangle die-cut didn't fully separate the backing on any card, press the Start button again for one more pass without unloading.
💡
About Rectangle Cut Depth With Cricut's own Printable Sticker Paper and the correct material setting, a single pass through the machine should cut cleanly through the backing. If the rectangle edges don't fully separate, run the mat through a second time by pressing the Start button again before unloading — this is preferable to stacking rectangle layers in the design.
  1. Place the mat face-down on a clean, flat surface and gently peel the mat away from the sticker sheet (rather than peeling the sheet off the mat). This prevents the sheet from curling.
  2. The sticker cards will now be fully separated along the rectangle cuts. Gently push them apart.
  3. Each student receives one card with their printed design. The kiss-cut sticker inside can be peeled away from the card backing cleanly.
  4. Distribute cards to students. Demonstrate how to peel the sticker from the backing if needed.
💡
Peeling Tip If stickers are tricky to peel at the edge, students can use a fingernail at the corner of the kiss-cut shape to lift the sticker away from the backing liner. The adhesive is permanent, so remind students to place stickers carefully.

Troubleshooting
Common Problems & Fixes
❌ Problem ⚠️ Likely Cause ✅ Fix
Cuts are misaligned from the printed design Attach not used on sticker group — layers shift on mat Rebuild each sticker group: select PNG + offset + rectangle → Attach. Reprint and cut.
Machine cuts in the completely wrong place Print Then Cut calibration not completed or outdated Re-run calibration: Menu → Settings → Machines → Machine Calibration → Print Then Cut.
Sheet won't print or triggers an error in Design Space Objects outside the printable boundary; red ⚠ in Layers Click the red ⚠ icon → choose Auto-Resize, or manually move/resize stickers until warnings clear.
Kiss cut goes all the way through the backing Wrong material setting — using full-cut setting instead of sticker paper Select material: Printable Sticker Paper, White (correct version for your liner). Try Less Pressure if still too deep.
Rectangle die-cut doesn't go fully through the backing Blade slightly dull or insufficient pressure for backing thickness Press the Start button again without unloading to run a second pass. If persistent, check blade sharpness and confirm the correct material setting is selected.
Sticker edges or offset border look wrong / incomplete Offset applied inconsistently or with a very small value Re-apply Offset per sticker individually; use 0.05"–0.1" and set fill to White. Reprint.
Material tears or rips during cutting Incorrect material setting, damaged blade, or sheet not flat on mat Confirm Printable Sticker Paper, White is selected. Replace blade if worn. Ensure sheet is fully flat with no lifted edges before loading.
Letters / elements of a PNG are each cut out separately PNG was saved as a cut-only file — Design Space treats each colour region as a separate cut path instead of printing the whole image Delete the layer, re-upload the PNG, and on the final save screen choose Print Then Cut Image (not Cut Image). This preserves all colours as a single printable layer that cuts around the outer edge only.
"No printer found" message in Design Space Unfamiliar printer devices in Windows settings Go to Windows Settings → Bluetooth & Devices → Printers & Scanners → remove any unrecognised printer entries. Return to Design Space and try again.
Sticker paper jams in the printer Printing from the wrong tray, or loading multiple sheets Remove all paper from the tray, load only one sheet of sticker paper at a time. Use the bypass/manual tray if available. Set printer to thick/heavyweight paper type.
